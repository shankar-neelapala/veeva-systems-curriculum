Football, or soccer, is the world’s most popular sport, featuring two teams of 11 players aiming to score by maneuvering a ball into the opponent’s net without using hands or arms (except for goalkeepers).

It emphasizes agility, stamina, and teamwork, with major competitions including the FIFA World Cup and UEFA Champions.

Gameplay & Rules: Matches are played on a rectangular field, typically 100-110 meters long, with the objective of scoring more goals than the opponent. The game is governed by the International Football Association Board, and matches are controlled by a referee.

Key Skills: Players focus on dribbling, passing, shooting, and ball control.

Positions: Teams consist of a goalkeeper, defenders, midfielders, and forwards.

Popularity: It is recognized as the most popular sport globally, both in terms of participants and spectators.

Major Tournaments: The FIFA World Cup is the most anticipated event, held every four years. Other, annual 
competitions include the Champions League, where top clubs compete for the UEFA trophy.

Cultural Impact: Football is a global phenomenon that teaches discipline, strategy, and resilience, while fostering strong community bonds.


Origins: Aspects of the game date back to the 2nd and 3rd centuries AD in China.
Modern Game: The modern rules of association football were formalized in 19th-century England.
Global Spread: The sport spread across Europe and to the rest of the world through British colonization

Transfer Window: A period (summer/January) when clubs buy and sell players.
Offside: A rule designed to prevent cherry-picking by forward players.
Goal Area: The small rectangular area in front of the goal where the goalie often plays
